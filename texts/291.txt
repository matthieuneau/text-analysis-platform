in preparation for christmas, i read stephen nissenbaum's 1998 " the battle for christmas, " a thorough exploration of this season. the book's title will be deceiving, because it has nothing to do with the recent sacred - vs. - secular christmas quarrels. nissenbaum explores the myriad ways that christmas has evolved in our nation. it turns out we've been jockeying for more than 300 years over what this holiday means. in colonial america our faith - filled ancestors banned christmas altogether, outlawing it in some colonies. until the 1760s, one could not even find an almanac that would print the word " christmas " on the date dec. 25. this opposition was because christmas had become a drunken spectacle where gangs of poor young men roamed the streets, making merry and engaging in acts of petty rowdyism, vaguely like today's new year's eve. it was customary and permissible for these gangs to knock on doors of strangers to demand gifts.
